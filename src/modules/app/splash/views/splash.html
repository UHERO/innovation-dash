Tracking Hawaii's Progress in Job Growth, Business Climate, and Economic Development

The nature of innovation is multi-dimensional, and so are the factors that feed into innovation activities. UHERO, in collaboration with the Hawaii Business Roundtable, Dev League, and Startup Paradise have created this interactive dashboard to summarize the status of Hawaii's innovation economy, and update the community on the features of this economy that are relevant to them.

Education

The main ingredient required for Hawaii’s innovation economy is a highly educated entrepreneurial labor force with the skill set appropriate for the industry segment within which they are trying to innovate.

Science and engineering graduate students per 1000 individuals 25-34 years old in 2011

11.1

The national average is 13.3.

In 2013, the percentage of Hawaii’s working population with a Bachelor’s degree or higher was greater than the national average.

Research &
Development

Hawaii’s investments in innovation initiatives must have proper linkages and address the entire pipeline of the innovation process from research all the way to commercialization and exportation of the product.

Average annual federal Small Business Innovation Research funding per $1M of GDP 2010-2012

$48

Or $3,350 total, which is above the national average. 15 states outperformed Hawaii.

In 2012, Hawaii spent $328,168 on Academic Science and Engineering R&D, which is greater than the US average.

Amount invested into Academic Science and Engineer R&D per $1000 of GDP

$4.53

The national average is $3.99 per $1000 of GDP.

Entrepreneurship

If we focus Hawaii’s resources on assisting entrepreneurial segments of our existing industries to embrace the global innovation economy, our investments to nurture our industries will be more effective.

1-year startup survival rate in 2012

79.2%

For the first time since 1996, Hawaii fell below the national average, which was 79.9% in 2012.

In 2012 Hawaii’s percentage of wokers employed by young firms (<5yrs old) or startups (<1yr old) was less than the US average.

Economic
Prosperity

Hawaii is ahead of other locales in attracting talent, the key ingredient to the innovation economy, because our name is synonymous with a high quality of life, which is a key deciding factor for innovation workers.

Average compensation per job in Hawaii in 2013

$52,151

This is an increase $12,484 since 2003.

In 2012, Hawaii’s workforce earned 20% less per job than the US average.

Average compensation per job in Hawaii in 2012

$44,518

The national average was $55,085.
